# Platform Positioning

## Core line

Start with one operating problem. Keep humans in control. Build AI that earns the right to expand.

## Shared narrative

NeuralMimicry should be presented as a technically serious platform company that helps buyers adopt AI in bounded, reviewable steps. The narrative is not "full autonomy everywhere". The narrative is controlled capability expansion anchored in specific problems, operating boundaries, and evidence.

## Messaging pillars

- Augmentation first: describe the products as force multipliers for accountable teams, not replacements for them.
- Minimum necessary access: highlight narrow scope, explicit permissions, and bounded authority.
- Governance alongside value: controls, audit, and operating evidence belong in the first deployment, not the third.
- One source of truth: planning, approvals, runtime state, and delivery evidence should stay reviewable.
- Sovereignty explicit: edge, data centre, and controlled cloud deployment options are part of the commercial position.

## Language to prefer

- governed
- reviewable
- bounded
- operator-visible
- explicit workflow choice
- closed-loop
- sovereign
- auditable
- staged rollout
- accountable humans

## Language to avoid

- autonomous everything
- black box intelligence
- replaces your team
- magical AI assistant
- AGI for the enterprise
- self-driving organisation

## Product framing

- Refiner: governed delivery and engineering workflow control
- Continuum: sovereign hybrid operations and infrastructure control
- Tracey: adaptive resilience with governed response
- AARNN: deeper adaptive intelligence when the use case justifies it
- Webots: embodied demonstration and browser-based experimentation tied back to AARNN assets

## Video rules

- Product videos should stay commercial, concrete, and problem-led.
- Training videos should assume a real operator and show sequence, prerequisites, and success criteria.
- Every script should include at least one governance or control proof point.
- If a claim is not explicit in the product brief or source map, flag it as a verification item instead of inventing it.
